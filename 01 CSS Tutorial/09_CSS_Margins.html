CSS Margins

p.one This p element has a top margin of 100px, a right margin of 150px, a bottom margin of 100px, and a left margin of 80px.

p.two margin: 125px 50px 75px 100px; /* 4 values: top, right, bottom, left */

p.three margin: 175px 50px 75px ; /* 3 values: top, right & left, bottom */

p.four margin: 100px 50px; /* 2 values: top & bottom, right & left */

p.five margin: 150px; /* 1 value: top & right & bottom & left */

p.six:You can set the margin property to auto to horizontally center the element within its container.
The element will then take up the specified width, and the remaining space will be split equally between the left and right margins

This paragraph has an inherited left margin (from the div element).

Top and bottom margins of elements are sometimes collapsed into a single margin that is equal to the largest of the two margins.
This does not happen on left and right margins! Only top and bottom margins !
